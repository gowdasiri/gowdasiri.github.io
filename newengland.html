New England in peak fall foliage

Every year I obsess over planning the perfect fall trip, hoping to catch nature at its autumn best. Three years in — arriving too early the first year to see only green, arriving late the next to find barren trees — I've learned some valuable lessons. This is the one where I finally got it right.

Planning

Timing is everything

No factor plays a larger role in making your leaf-peeping trip a success than planning ahead. I recommend using the Peak Foliage Map on the New England website for peak fall prediction — it's pretty spot on. Peak foliage lasts about a week, so make sure to book your hotel rooms and national park reservations well ahead of time.

Book ahead — seriously. In Maine, fall usually peaks on Columbus Day weekend and every small town in the vicinity will be booked solid. We once tried winging it and ended up driving three hours looking for a place to stay. On my latest trip, we couldn't drive up Cadillac Mountain in Acadia because the vehicle reservation sold out the day before we checked. Book early.

Navigation

Route options

Option 1 — Recommended

A progressive road trip moving through Maine, New Hampshire, and Vermont — staying at different spots each night to maximize time at each destination with minimal backtracking.

Option 2 — Base camp

If you prefer staying in one place and doing daily road trips, this route works well — but it involves significantly more driving. Good for those who hate packing and unpacking every night.

Day by day

5-Day Itinerary

Starting from NYC, this route takes you up the Maine coast, through Acadia National Park, across the White Mountains of New Hampshire, and into the villages of Vermont before looping home through the Berkshires.

Day 00

NYC → Boston

NYC Departure Boston Overnight

Leave from NYC by 5pm and stay at a hotel near Boston for the night. Pack dinner or grab it on route. Drive time: approximately 5 hours.

Day 01

Route 1 Drive — Portland & Camden

Cape Elizabeth Portland Head Lighthouse Old Port Portland Camden Acadia NP Echo Lake Beach Bass Harbor Lighthouse

OTD 8:30am. Make stops at Cape Elizabeth and Portland Head Lighthouse. You could also take a stroll around Old Port, Portland and grab some lobster rolls — fresh lobster in Maine is non-negotiable.

Enjoy the drive along Route 1 to Acadia NP. Break for lunch at Camden — a town cute as a button. We had some of the best Asian food there at Long Grain (I know what you're thinking — Asian food in Maine? Take my word for it).

Reach Acadia by 4pm (5hr 10min drive). Head over to Echo Lake Beach in the evening and enjoy sunset at the Bass Harbor Lighthouse.

Day 02

Acadia National Park

2 Cats Bar Harbor Beehive Trail Jordan Cliffs Sand Beach Thundering Hole Jordan Pond South Bubble Trail Bar Harbor

Breakfast: Start your day with eggs and tea at 2 Cats, Bar Harbor.

Morning: Beehive Trail — steep and exhilarating — or the Jordan Cliffs Trail if you want something more adventurous. Hack for Beehive: the descent is a much easier hike with large stone steps, so if you're afraid of heights, you can simply summit from the downward trail path.

Afternoon: Sand Beach and Thundering Hole. Grab lunch at Jordan Pond Restaurant within the park — worth it for the views alone.

Post-lunch: South Bubble Trail or Beech Ladder Trail. On route to trailheads, you'll drive through a large stretch of the scenic 27-mile Park Loop Road. Don't rush it — stop and take it all in.

Evening: Bar Harbor for dinner and drinks.

Note: You cannot rent bikes or canoes within the park.

Day 03

Mount Washington & Kancamagus Highway, New Hampshire

Cadillac Mountain Sunrise Pink Pastry Shop Mt Washington Cog Railway Kancamagus Pass Burlington, VT

Catch the sunrise from the top of Cadillac Mountain — the highest point on the Eastern Seaboard, named after the French explorer who went on to found Detroit (yes, that Cadillac). The early start helps get the long drive underway. You need to make a vehicle reservation online ahead of time.

Stop by the Pink Pastry Shop, Bar Harbor, for the most fluffy blueberry lemon whoopie pies (they run out by noon — get there early). Blueberries are a Maine specialty; fields are usually open for picking from late July to mid-September.

Head to New Hampshire for a ride on the Mount Washington Cog Railway (drive time: 4hr 30min) — taking you to the top of the highest peak in the Northeastern US at 6,288 ft. The train is 45 minutes each way with about an hour at the summit. Check the weather: shelter at the top is sparse. The drive itself winds through White Mountain National Forest.

Arguably the best way to see wide swaths of fall foliage is to explore NH and Vermont via scenic byways — secondary roads winding past Victorian towns, mountain summits, and lakes. Watch for roadside antique shops, farm stands with fresh apples and pumpkins, covered bridges, and peregrine falcons overhead. These drives were the absolute highlight of the trip.

If you're up for it, head to Burlington, Vermont for dinner and drinks.

Drive through the Kancamagus Pass if time permits — it's known to be absolutely stunning in peak fall.

Day 04

Stowe, Vermont

Zip-lining, Stowe Mountain Mount Mansfield Cold Hollow Cider Mill Von Trapp Brewery Camel's Hump Alpaca Farm

The village of Stowe, Vermont — with its quaint shops, breweries, and restaurants all set against a gorgeous mountain backdrop — is one of the most beloved towns in the state.

Fall is a great time to experience zip-lining at Stowe Mountain Lodge. Launching from the top of Mount Mansfield via Gondola, this is one of the longest and fastest zip-line tours in North America. Three zip line spans and a bird's-eye view of peak foliage.

Drive along Route 108 up Stowe Mountain Lodge — a local tip — to surround yourself with the most breathtaking fall foliage. Also a state park, so hiking shoes open up further options.

Wind your way up the historic Toll Road to the summit ridge of Mount Mansfield, Vermont's highest peak at 4,393 ft. Worth the fee — views of Lake Champlain and the Adirondack Mountains from the top.

No trip to Vermont is complete without a stop at Cold Hollow Cider Mill for fresh-pressed apple cider and their legendary cider donuts. They turn out over 800 dozen a day in fall. Watch them press it right in front of you and grab some apple butter or a preserved maple leaf as a souvenir.

Head to Berkshires for the night (drive time: 3hr 30min).

If you love farms and animals — especially alpacas — head to Camel's Hump Alpaca Farm. $15 per person to spend time with the herd. They're docile and won't bite, but don't look them in the eye or they'll spit at you. Truly. Also, the von Trapp Brewery & Bierhall at Trapp Family Lodge is worth a visit for authentic Austrian lagers brewed with Vermont spring water.

Day 05

Head Back to NYC

Berkshires, MA Housatonic River Apple Picking NYC

Break your journey at Berkshires, MA if the drive feels too long. Go kayaking down the Housatonic River on a guided tour with Berkshire Canoe Tours. Or check out Mount Greylock or Monument Mountain for one last dose of fall foliage.

If you love being on a farm, apple picking (available July–October) makes for a lovely stop on the way home. Drive time back to NYC: approximately 3 hours.

Preparation

Packing list

Pack for cold, rain, and lots of hiking. Fall in New England means unpredictable weather and wet trails.

  • Raincoat — Non-negotiable. Pack one you can layer under.
  • Waterproof boots — For trails and wet leaves. Ankle support recommended for Acadia.
  • Active wear & hiking shoes — Required for Acadia and White Mountain National Forest.
  • Windbreaker jacket — Summit winds and coastal areas can be biting even in October.
  • Layers — Mornings and evenings get cold quickly; midday can be surprisingly warm.
  • Extra bag for souvenirs — You'll want room for maple syrup, apple butter, cider, and more.
Eating & drinking

Food highlights

New England's fall food scene is one of its best features — seasonal produce, farm-fresh cider, legendary seafood, and some genuinely surprising meals along the way.

Lobster rolls

While in Portland (or Maine in general) you have to try fresh lobster. Lobster rolls are the quintessential sandwich of New England. Non-negotiable.

Apple cider donuts

Cold Hollow Cider Mill in Stowe has been making their legendary donuts from a secret recipe for 25 years — over 800 dozen a day in peak fall. Get them warm.

Blueberry whoopie pies

Blueberry lemon whoopie pies at Pink Pastry Shop, Bar Harbor. They run out by noon. Set your alarm.

Asian food in Camden

Long Grain in Camden, Maine. I know it sounds unlikely. It was one of the best meals of the trip. Just trust it.

Looking back

Highlights of the trip

01

The drives. Scenic byways through New Hampshire and Vermont in peak fall — winding roads past covered bridges, farm stands, and forests on fire with color. Nothing prepares you for it.

02

Acadia National Park. From the Beehive Trail to the South Bubble and Jordan Pond, every trail delivered. The Park Loop Road alone is worth the trip.

03

Sunrise at Cadillac Mountain. The highest point on the Eastern Seaboard — the first place in the US to see the sunrise. Watching it from the summit in fall is something else entirely.

04

The alpacas. Camel's Hump Alpaca Farm in Stowe. $15 per person. Every single cent worth it. (Don't look them in the eye.)

05

The sunset en route Vermont. Driving west, the colors were wilder in person than any photograph could capture.

06

Cold Hollow Cider Mill. Watching fresh cider get pressed, sampling from the tank, eating a warm cider donut on the spot. A perfect fall moment.

"Three years of trying, one perfect week of peak foliage, alpacas, apple cider donuts, and the most spectacular drives of my life. New England in fall is worth the planning — just book ahead."
