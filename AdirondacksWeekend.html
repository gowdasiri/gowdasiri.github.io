Itinerary

Day 1:

Morning: NYC -> Lake Placid (5 hours)

  • Start your day by driving to Lake Placid, a charming village nestled in the heart of the Adirondack Mountains. Take NYS-73 High Peaks Scenic Byway to get there, although every road in that region is a scenic byway, so you really can't go wrong whichever road you choose to take.

Afternoon:

  • Enjoy a leisurely lunch in one of the local restaurants in Lake Placid. The area offers a variety of cuisines to suit different tastes. Luna Restaurant on Main Street serves up some great vodka pasta,
  • High Falls Gorge - An easy access 20 minute walk to view four stunning waterfalls. No pets allowed on the trail (Open 9AM - 4:30 AM). Tickets are $16 for adults.

Evening:

  • Head to Mirror Lake for a relaxing evening. You can rent a kayak, canoe, or paddleboard to explore the tranquil waters or simply relax by the shore.
  • Stroll through the main street of Lake Placid and browse the unique shops and boutiques.
  • Have dinner at one of the local restaurants and enjoy the cozy mountain atmosphere.
  • If you're interested in nightlife, check out the local bars and pubs for live music or a relaxed evening.

Day 2:

Morning:

  • Wake up early and drive to the High Peaks region of the Adirondacks. This area is known for its stunning mountain vistas and numerous hiking trails.
  • Choose a trail based on your hiking experience and preferences. Popular options include Mount Jo Loop Trail, Mount Marcy, Algonquin Peak, Big Crow Mountain (shorter hike) and Cascade Mountain (5 hours). Make sure to check trail conditions and weather forecasts before starting your hike.

Afternoon:

  • Enjoy a picnic lunch amidst the breathtaking natural beauty of the Adirondacks. Many trailheads have designated picnic areas where you can relax and refuel.
  • If you're up for more adventure, consider visiting one of the nearby lakes for swimming or paddling. Some popular options include Heart Lake, Avalanche Lake, or Saranac Lake.
  • Adirondack Loj trails : Stop at Heart Lake before taking a sunset hike up Mount Jo trail.
  • We tried the Mount Jo Trail - the 2 mile loop trail takes an average of 1 h 19 min to complete. This is a very popular area for hiking, so you'll likely encounter other people while exploring. The trail is open year-round and is beautiful to visit anytime. Dogs are welcome, but must be on a leash.

Evening:

  • Take a scenic drive to the town of Saranac Lake, known for its outdoor recreational opportunities and charming downtown area. Explore the shops and galleries in downtown Saranac Lake.
  • Lake Placid Pub & Brewery: If you're a beer enthusiast, don't miss a visit to the Lake Placid Pub & Brewery. Sample their award-winning craft beers and enjoy delicious pub fare in a lively atmosphere. Most restaurants in these towns close by 9pm at the latest, so plan accordingly.

Day 3: Lake Placid -> NYC (4 hr 30 mins)

Morning:

  • Short hike for sunrise is Owl's Head Mountain near Lake Placid, spend time by the lake paddle boarding or hydro biking.

Drive back to Manhattan.

Lake Placid town, overlooking Mirror Lake and Lake Placid, is charming, quaint and everything you'd imagine a town nestled in these mountains to be.

  1. Bookstore Plus : My personal favorite store in this town! I was looking to buy the book Shoe Dog, and they just happened to have it on sale. They even have a book club! Cool place for souvenirs too!
  2. Lake Placid Pub & Brewery : If you're a beer enthusiast, don't miss a visit to the Lake Placid Pub & Brewery. Sample their award-winning craft beers and enjoy delicious pub fare in a lively atmosphere.
  3. Adirondacks Chocolates, Adirondack Popcorn & Co & Emma's Creamery : These cute little shops on Main Street serve up yummy snacks and desserts. Emma's Creamery even serves Tipsy Scoop ice cream! Coffee Bar was another little highlight overlooking Mirror Lake - serving some great coffee and even better vibes.
  4. Breakfast and Co. : Nowhere better for a power packed breakfast than this cute restaurant by Mirror Lake. They served some great omelets, hash browns and coffee.
  5. Adirondack Store : Great place for souvenirs, they have a canoe hanging on the ceiling, a waterfall feature and maple syrups from neighboring Vermont!

This town also has a movie theatre which runs shows Thursdays to Sundays each week.
