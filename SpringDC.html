Spring in DC

June 7, 2024 • Travel

BEST TIME TO VISIT

While DC is lovely to visit most of the year, spring (March-May) for cherry blossoms and fall (September-November) are definite highlights. I'm particularly biased towards spring, especially if you're able to track peak cherry blossom bloom in the Tidal Basin. It's breathtaking and magical to watch blossoms in pink, white and all colors in between in the basin that is transformed into a cherry blossom wonderland.

Out of all my trips to DC, there's only been one time where I've been able to catch peak bloom. It's worth noting that it gets extremely crowded during that time though, so beware.

TRANSPORTATION

To get from place to place in DC by public transportation, you’ll need to get your hands on a SmarTrip card. These are sold at any Metro station around the city for $2, and you can reload them as needed for trips. Trips have a flat fare of $2 on weekends and a variable rate depending on distance from $1-$6 during the weekdays. You can use the card on any public bus or metro line in the city.

Cabs are easily available too on Uber, Lyft, etc. and are another easy way to get around the city.

ITINERARY

DAY 1

8:00 AM. Board the train from Penn Station NYC - it’s a three and a half hour ride with several stops along the way in NJ, Philly and Baltimore.

11:30 AM. Reach Union Station DC.

12:00 PM. Grab lunch at Union Station or in the neighborhood.

1:00 PM. Tour the Capitol Hill neighborhood and explore the U.S. Capitol, Library of Congress and Supreme Court. The neighborhood is also home to the Folger Shakespeare Library, which houses the largest collection of Shakespeare memorabilia outside of England.

You can buy same day timed entry tickets to the Library of Congress on their website. For someone who loves books, it was really cool to tour through the library and see the collection on display and learn about the history of their incredible collection. Even with timed entry tickets, the lines were long, with about a half hour wait.

4:00 PM. Check into the hotel/Airbnb.

5:00 PM. Head to Georgetown neighborhood. There's lots to see around here, but honestly it's just as nice to walk around and get lost in this beautiful neighborhood. If you like to plan like me though, here's a list of spots worth checking out :

  • Bridge Street Bookstore - Just a nice little bookstore to start off the exploration
  • Trailhead C&O Canal Towpath - Head down the Canal Towpath next to the next spot.
  • Baked & Wired - Located near the canal, this bakery/coffee shop has plenty of sweet treats and delicious coffees. Seems to be extremely popular among locals and tourists alike. Disclaimer : it was a little too sweet for my taste.
  • Georgetown Waterfront Park - Head to the park next and enjoy the views and the breeze for a while, before heading back up in the direction of the Exorcist steps.
  • Exorcist steps - While I haven't watched this movie, and don't plan to to be honest, these steps were featured in a novel I read recently called Yellow Face. So I was curious to see the steps.
  • Georgetown University - A short walk from the steps, this university building is quite grand and majestic and definitely worth a quick popover to check out. The streets around are lined with colorful houses and made a nice wander. Even came across a graffiti wall with The Wave art.
  • Shopping - Head back down to main street for a little shopping maybe? Even the H&M store looked exclusive on this street.
  • Yellow - We got dinner at this Levantine restaurant by Michelin starred Chef Michael Rafidi. Recommend checking out this place for brunch/lunch though to savor their Za’atar and Labne Croissant, cubed potato fries and falafel wrap. It was featured on Somebody Feed Phil.

Another noteworthy cupcake shop mention : Georgetown Cupcake. Famous for its TV show on TLC, DC Cupcakes, expect a long line. Enjoy typical flavors like red velvet and vanilla, but also enjoy their seasonal cupcakes like the unicorn cupcake in March or the pumpkin spice cupcake in October!

DAY 2

9 AM . Start the day of exploration early with some breakfast.

  • DC’s bagel scene has heated up. Call Your Mother has locations in many parts of the city is has some great bagel sandwiches.
  • If you're visiting on a weekend, visit the farmers' markets at Dupont Circle or Eastern Market on Capitol Hill. The blueberry buckwheat pancakes at Eastern Market are a local favorite. 

10:00 AM. Start with museums and galleries. The National Museum of Natural History is the most popular of the Smithsonian institutions.

Some other museums worth checking out :

  • National Portrait Gallery - The Mall is home to a host of other museums, including the National Gallery of Art, where the U.S.'s only Leonardo Da Vinci painting hangs, while the National Archives, which houses the original Constitution and Declaration of Independence, is a must for any American history buff.
  • National Archives Museum - If this is your first time in DC, definitely prioritize this museum. After all, it houses the Declaration of Independence, the Constitution, and the Bill of Rights. These are documents that are really neat to see and well worth the long lines.
  • National Gallery of Art - From Renaissance to Rococo, the National Gallery of Art has it all. If you love art and want to explore the works of Degas, the Greeks, and Warhol, this is a great museum to spend a couple of hours in. 

12:00 PM. Lunch at Rasika.

2:30 PM. Old Town Alexandria: Gorgeous architecture, charming walking streets, and historic landmarks welcome you in Old Town Alexandria. The Yellow metro line is an easy way to get there from downtown, and there's a free Trolley you can use to get around Old Town.

  • King Street Mile: Check out boutique shops along the way, and perhaps grab an ice cream at Pop’s Old Fashioned Ice Cream Shop or coffee at Mischa's coffee shop.
  • Check out Old Town Alexandria’s Historic Spots! Alexandria was home to many famous haunts of founding fathers like these:
    • Gadsby’s Tavern Museum: Discover where George Washington dined including the first 5 US presidents. You can even dine at the restaurant or just visit the museum! ($5 for adults)
    • Carlyle House: Visit an 18th-century mansion built by one of Alexandria’s founders.($7 for adults)
    • George Washington Townhouse: Even though Mount Vernon was only a few miles away, he had a spot in Alexandria. This smaller house was reconstructed and sits where the original once stood, and you can spot his bust in the window!
    • Torpedo Factory Art Centre - Collection of artists showcasing & selling paintings, photos & other pieces in a former factory.
    • Spite House - It is said that the 300 sqft house was built literally out of spite (due to disagreements with the neighbors maybe?) in an alleyway.

6:00 PM. Walk Down the National Mall: Head back to DC in the evening. The National Mall is lined with museums and bookended by the Capitol and the Washington Monument. Start at the Whitehouse and make your way down towards the Washington Monument.

7:30 PM. Spend Sunset at the Lincoln Memorial: Enjoying sunset at the Lincoln Memorial is a real highlight.

DAY 3

9:00 AM Get Some Fresh Air at the Tidal Basin: The Tidal Basin is home to the Jefferson Memorial, Martin Luther King Memorial and gorgeous cherry trees, all surrounding a small waterfront that’s an offshoot of the Potomac River. It’s the perfect place for a morning stroll, especially in the springtime.

11:00 AM Brunch. Did you even go to DC, if you didn't get brunch there?

Some noteworthy options include :

  • Le Diplomat
  • Farmers Fishers Bakers
  • Compass Rose

12:30 PM. US Capitol tour - Home to the House and Congress, the tour of the Capitol was a standout. All tours are led by our professional tour guides and visit the Crypt, the Rotunda and National Statuary Hall. The tour does not include the Senate and House Galleries.

Same day timed entry tour tickets are available, so make sure to book in advance. There were no lines or any wait. The security is tight, so make sure to check what you're allowed to take (food and beverages are not allowed in some places).

3:30 PM. Late lunch at Family Ethiopian Restaurant or CherCher.

Ethiopian food is an integral part of D.C.’s restaurant scene. Refugees who fled their war-ravaged African nation in the 1970s and ’80s settled in the District and kept their culinary traditions alive. Initially rooted around Little Ethiopia at the intersection of Ninth and U Streets NW, the Ethiopian community continues to spread out to parts of Northern Virginia and Maryland. Silver Spring hosts the D.C. area’s biggest Ethiopian cultural festival once a year. (Eater )

5:00 PM. Catch the Amtrack back to NYC.
