Day trip to Beacon NY

June 7, 2024 • Travel

Nestled in the heart of the Hudson Valley, just a scenic train ride away from the hustle and bustle of New York City, lies the charming riverside town of Beacon. With its vibrant arts scene, picturesque landscapes, and eclectic shops and eateries, Beacon offers a perfect escape for a day trip or even a weekend full of exploration and relaxation.

Take the Metro North Hudson line from Grand Central Terminal to Beacon. The train route is by the Hudson River the entire way, which makes for an extremely scenic ride!

Tip : Make sure to sit by the window on the three seater seat for breathtaking views of the Hudson.

On reaching the Beacon train station, you can either walk to the Main Street (about 15 mins away and slightly uphill) or take a cab up there.

Tip : The B-Free shuttle is a free shuttle that connects the train station, DIA Beacon, Main Street and the trail head. It's a convenient way to get around town.

Stroll Beacon's Main Street

Leisurely stroll along Beacon's Main Street, lined with quaint boutiques, art galleries, and cozy cafes. Stop by the Beacon Farmer's Market to sample some locally sourced produce and artisanal goods, and perhaps pick up a handmade souvenir to remember your trip by.

Highlights :

  • Coffee from Bank Sq. Coffee - You're greeted by Bank Sq. Coffee, a local coffee shop at the start of Main Street, and it's a great place to start your day with a nice cup of coffee and snacks.
  • Hudson Glass Blowing - When I planned a trip here, I had only just read Book Lovers, and was in love with the city girl in a small town trope. Sweet Home Alabama was my favorite romcom movie at the time. So I was hyping up Beacon to be this small town, and lo and behold being greeted with a glass blowing factory in town. I mean, how many small towns even have those?? Anyway, it's a beautiful store offering glass blowing classes too (make sure to pre-book). They even have a studio upstairs where you can watch artists at work. It's all very cool and romantic!
  • Bookstores - This town has quite a few surprisingly. I recommend Binnacle Books and Stanza Books. Binnacle books also supports local prisons by encouraging visitors to buy books from the list of requested books that they then mail to the prison. Stanza is simply lovely, with a couch in front of a fireplace where you can sit and browse through a book, like a cozy little library.
  • Clothing stores - Beacon is a hippie town for sure. With an endless line of vintage stores with a really tasteful collection, we had a great time browsing most of these - Blackbird Attic, and many more! Hyberbole is another store I love visiting when I'm here, with really cute stuff.
  • Art Galleries - Christopher Stella and Maria Lago to name a couple.
  • More stores -
  • Glazed Over Donuts - Fresh hot delicious donuts made to order at this local donut shop. Recommend the lemon donut with vanilla drizzle topped with coconut flakes.

Hike Mount Beacon

Embark on a hike up Mount Beacon for breathtaking views of the Hudson River and surrounding valley. The trailhead is just a short drive from the town center and offers varying levels of difficulty, so whether you're a seasoned hiker or a novice explorer, there's a route for you. You can easily take a cab to the trail head or even hike there.

Explore the many restaurants & Breweries

After working up an appetite, head to one of Beacon's many charming eateries for a delicious lunch. From farm-to-table fare to ethnic cuisine, the town boasts a diverse culinary scene sure to satisfy any palate. Be sure to try some locally brewed beer or cider for a true taste of the Hudson Valley.

  • Momo Valley - I would make a trip down to Beacon just to dine here. Hands down the best Nepali food I've had so far - the food is soulful, spicy and absolutely delicious! I tried to get them to sell their hot sauce to me, because it really is out of the world! Recommend the dal bhat tarkari and the veggie chowmein.
  • Ziatun - Mediterranean restaurant with fantastic food! Try their falafel burger with za'atar fries!
  • Mezlingah Tap House - Nice bar to wind down and have some great cocktails after a long day of hiking and exploring. Happy Valley Arcade bar is another cool bar with arcade games like Pac Man on Main Street.
  • Hudson Brewery - Hudson valley is known for it's many brewaries. So make sure to try a couple if you've got time. Bonus : This particular one is located by the river.
  • Beacon Bread & Company - Local cafe, from the same owner as Ziatun, serving freshly baked bread and desserts. Also a nice place to get breakfast. Beacon Falls Cafe and Crepes place are also nice breakfast options.

DIA Beacon - Art Museum

Take a stroll along the Hudson River waterfront and explore the Dia Beacon, a renowned contemporary art museum housed in a former Nabisco factory. Featuring works by some of the world's most celebrated artists, including Richard Serra and Louise Bourgeois, the museum offers a thought-provoking cultural experience unlike any other. The entrance is about $30 and they offer free tours of the museum.

From its vibrant arts scene to its scenic natural beauty, Beacon, New York, offers a perfect blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation for a memorable day trip. Whether you're a nature lover, art enthusiast, or foodie, there's something for everyone to enjoy in this hidden gem of the Hudson Valley. So why not escape the city for a day and discover all that Beacon has to offer?
