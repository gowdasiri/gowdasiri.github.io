In a day and age when everyone with access to the internet is operating on autopilot to some degree, Frederik Riedel’s app reminds you to take a deep breath, pause and rethink your intention before opening an app. This simple yet ingenious idea really helps you to reduce the amount of time you spend on social media apps by allowing you to mindfully use the app and enforces scrolling with a purpose. 

Many studies and books written about behavior reiterate the role friction plays in both adoption and continued engagement. By introducing friction, One Sec reinforces conscious usage that most other apps simplify to allow unconscious trigger and usage. 

“Once our habits become automatic, we stop paying attention to what we are doing. The process of behavior change always starts with awareness. You need to be aware of your habits before you can change them.(page 67)

The Four Laws of Behavior Change are a simple set of rules we can use to build better habits. They are (1) make it obvious  (2) make it attractive (3) make it easy (4) make it satisfying.(page 55)

To build a habit you need to practice it. And the most effective way to make the practice happen is to adhere to the 3rd Law of Behavior Change : make it easy. (page 147)” - excerpts from Atomic Habits by James Clear

“In his book Something Really New : Three Simple Steps to Creating Truly Innovative Products author Denis J. Hauptly deconstructs the process of innovation into its most fundamental steps. First Hauptly states, understand the reason people use a product service. Next, lay out the steps the customer must take to get the job done. Finally, once the series of tasks from intention to outcome is understood, simply start removing the steps until you reach the simplest possible process. For Hauptly, easier equals better.”  - Hooked by Nir Eyal emphasizes the importance of making habits easier.

This app does exactly the opposite of that. A simple solution to a rather complicated problem. By adding friction, not only does it reduce the craving to open the app every ten seconds, but also introduces mindfulness and allows you to work the app rather than the prediction algorithm in the app to work you. 

Features

Set up on a app is easy enough and it's only feature is it gets triggered on opening the host app. The screen asks you to take a deep breath as it introduces a pause.

Metrics 

Measuring success for this app would be directly linked to the usage of the app it is being set up on. 

  • Reduced time spent on the app for which automation is set up to trigger one sec, is the north star metric in this case. 
  • Reduced number of times the app was opened is another indicator. This indicates that the unconscious trigger is now more conscious, since we are conscious of when we open it. 
  • Given that intentional usage is the main use case, gathering customer feedback to evaluate if they feel that the pause enables intentional usage, would also be a metric to consider. 

Improvements 

I think an improvement would be to have One Sec set up after a fixed amount of time of using the app, for instance, every five minutes of Instagram, where you are forced to take a deep breath and think about your intention. Since the app does make you aware of the number of times you opened it and the overall time spent, most of us tend to use the app longer than we intended generally without realizing it and this could be a barrier to that.
