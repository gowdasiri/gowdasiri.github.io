Rohtang Pass — Kullu Valley, Himachal Pradesh

With a 4 a.m. wake-up call, a lingering fever, and barely any sleep — we went anyway. Some trips are like that.

My friends and I were sick from something we'd had the last evening (read: special hot chocolate) and had barely slept. Since we'd looked forward to going here for ages, we decided to push through. The pass had opened to tourists that very day for the season — which meant we got the first glimpse of untouched snow, clear roads, and the best weather since summer had just begun.

The beauty we were surrounded by had a medicinal effect on us — the remnants of the fever simply evaporated.

The drive to the pass was one of the most scenic I've ever been on. Winding hilly roads, through streams (loved this part), snow-capped mountains on the horizon, and a full view of the beautiful Kullu valley spread out below. It was postcard-perfect everywhere we looked. Cameras out, snapping away, old Hindi songs playing in the background — our three-hour drive to the pass was a road trip to remember.

If you've been to Ladakh or Kashmir, you probably know what to expect. For me and my friends, this was our first trip to the North. We arrived exhausted from the lack of sleep, famished from skipping breakfast, and severely dehydrated — but none of that mattered as we reached the magnificent Rohtang valley. Worth noting: the government has banned snow activities like skiing, and being the first day the pass was open, there were crowds. But the views alone are reason enough.

A trip to Himachal is incomplete without at least a few of the many breathtaking valleys the state has to offer. This is one you shouldn't miss.

From the road

"We arrived sick, sleep-deprived, and starving. We left completely changed. That's the thing about the mountains — they have a way of making everything else irrelevant."
