Ten things to do at the Wizarding World of Harry Potter — because the real magic is in the details.

10 things to do

01

Adopt a Pygmy Puff at Weasley's Wizarding Wheezes

I know what you're thinking — if you wanted a pet, you'd rather get an owl or maybe a toad at the Magical Menagerie. They're low maintenance, and an owl would save you some serious wizarding money on mail. And if I'm being completely honest, I got myself an owl before I even considered a Pygmy. However, adopting one of these pink fur balls at Weasley's Wizarding Wheezes involves a naming ceremony (I named her Rosie, and she's as cute as can be) which made it entirely worth it.

The ceremony lasts about two minutes, pulls you into the world of magic, and the witch conducting it educates you on what goes into owning a Pygmy — they eat earwax once a day and will act up if you mistreat them. It was far more enjoyable than just buying an owl and was, hands down, the highlight of my Wizarding World trip.

02

Grab a butterbeer or two at the Hopping Pot

Obvious, I know. Butterbeer is found everywhere — the Hopping Pot, Three Broomsticks, Leaky Cauldron, street vendors. Beyond that: ice cream at Florean Fortescue's Ice Cream Parlour, Firewhisky at Hog's Head, pumpkin juice. They're all delicious and together they make for a genuinely fun eating tour of both parks.

03

Watch the light show on Hogwarts Castle

We visited during Christmas, and they had a light show every half hour from 6pm on the Hogwarts Castle near Hogsmeade (Islands of Adventure). It was quite a spectacle. Set aside an evening visit to Hogsmeade specifically for this — it's worth staying late.

04

Exchange money at Gringotts

The very first thing I wanted to do in Diagon Alley. Gringotts is impossible to miss — a fire-breathing dragon guards it from above. Exchanging muggle currency for Gringotts Wizarding Bank Notes is oddly satisfying and genuinely fun. You can use the bills alongside regular cash at most park vendors. Any leftover notes can be exchanged back at the end of your visit, or kept as a souvenir.

05

Catch a Celestina Warbeck performance in Diagon Alley

She's wonderfully talented — and pay close attention to the lyrics, because they're genuinely hilarious. We ended up catching several performances over the two days we spent at the parks. It's very easy to see why she's the most well-known singer in the Wizarding World.

06

Explore Knockturn Alley

An easy one to miss — keep your eyes open. The alley features the iconic Borgin and Burkes store, stocked with some of the most unsettling artifacts ever assembled. Pay attention to the basement window: a green light flashes every few minutes. And if you scored an Outstanding in Defense Against the Dark Arts, you'll know there's only one spell that produces green flames. Listen closely at the right moment. You'll hear something interesting — I won't say more.

07

Treat yourself at Honeydukes

Pure sugar heaven. Chocolate Frogs, Pumpkin Cake, Cauldron Cake — and about a thousand other sweets. Don't go overboard, because the temptation is very real. Bertie Bott's Every Flavour Beans deserve a special mention — brilliantly creative with the flavors, and guessing each one as you go is half the fun. The quintessential booger and vomit flavors sit alongside perfectly normal banana and cherry. Quite a treat.

08

Ride the Hogwarts Express

An obvious one, but worth flagging: some 2-park passes — including ours — don't include the Hogwarts Express. We had to purchase a separate $62 ticket to ride the train and access both parks in one day. Absolutely worth looking into before you buy your park tickets. The ride itself is full of lovely details and small surprises along the way.

09

Wand show at Ollivander's

If you thought you could simply go in and pick whichever wand you wanted — think again. The wand picks the wizard, Dear Reader. Catch Ollivander's show before you step into the shop for wand shopping. It's a magical little performance that brings back everything about Harry's first visit to the original Ollivander's.

10

Send a postcard from the Owl Post

A little frustrating, honestly — as someone who buys a postcard as a souvenir everywhere she goes, it was a blow to discover that Universal only sells postcards in packs of ten. Still, it's genuinely fun to have your card stamped at the Owl Post in Hogsmeade and Diagon Alley. Send your Harry Potter fan club friends greetings from the Wizarding World — they'll love it.

⚠ Spoilers below

Pay attention to the details everywhere you go. The paintings talk, obviously. The mirror at Madam Malkin's offers lovely compliments. The Fat Lady is as fussy as ever. You can hear reporters hard at work from outside the doors of the Daily Prophet. We ran into a Ministry witch carrying a crystal ball — invisible to us — and she never broke character. The wizard at Ollivander's told dark wizarding jokes and anecdotes. The dragon at Gringotts breathes actual fire. Self-stirring cauldrons. Self-knitting needles. Self-writing quills. Herbs you've only read about in books. And it goes on and on.

Just like everything else in life — it's all about the little things.

"This place is built for people who grew up with these books. Every corner has something in it for you — and the best moments are the ones you stumble into by accident."
