Havelock Island, Andaman & Nicobar Islands · India

Land of evergreen forests and white sand beaches. The first Indian territory to gain independence from the British. The Andaman Islands have played a significant role in the culture and history of India — and today, they're home to some of the most pristine waters and coral reefs in the world.

The main language spoken on the islands is Bengali. These islands are also home to sea turtles — a species whose population has declined due to fishing in the surrounding seas — alongside a remarkable variety of fish and aquatic wildlife.

Logistics

Getting there

Fly into Port Blair, then take a ferry to and from Havelock. Make sure to book your ferry well in advance — they fill up fast. We used ITT Majestic to get to Havelock and Macruise to get back. Neither had an outdoor passenger deck. Between the two, we preferred ITT Majestic.

On the island

Getting around the island

  • Auto-rickshaw — Slightly cheaper than taxi. Roads are quiet, even more so at night. Recommended to get back early if using autos after dark.
  • Taxi — More comfortable for longer distances or when carrying gear.
  • Scooter rental — The most convenient option. Lets you explore at your own pace and pull over wherever you want.
Things to do

Activities

Snorkelling & Island Hopping

The coral reefs around Havelock are some of the best in India. Boat tours take you between islands with stops to snorkel in clear, shallow water teeming with fish.

Scuba Diving

Nemo Point is one of the most popular dive sites. Multiple operators on the island offer guided dives for all levels, from first-timers to certified divers.

Kayaking

Kayak through the mangroves or along the coast at your own pace. A peaceful way to see parts of the island that boats can't reach.

Elephant Beach Hike

A jungle hike to a secluded beach. I didn't manage this one — it rained almost every day, and the marshes make it difficult when wet. Worth attempting in drier conditions.

Accommodation

Where to stay

Barefoot

Direct access to Radhanagar Beach — consistently ranked one of the best beaches in Asia. The property feels like you're staying in the middle of a forest. Unique and peaceful.

Sea Shell Havelock

A solid beach resort at a good price point. The Indian food here was incredible — genuinely one of the highlights of the trip. A reliable and comfortable base.

Taj

The premium option on the island for those who want a full luxury experience.

Looking back

Some highlights

01

Sunset at Radhanagar Beach. One of the best beaches in Asia, and a sunset that proves it. The sky turns extraordinary colors as the sun drops behind the treeline into the water.

02

The water. The turquoise color of Havelock's water looks unreal — like someone turned up the saturation on the world. Snorkelling through it is even better.

03

Kaala Paani — Cellular Jail, Port Blair. A powerful and sobering piece of history. The colonial-era prison where India's freedom fighters were jailed is now a national memorial. The sound and light show in the evening is particularly moving.

04

The food at Sea Shell. I didn't expect this to make the highlights list, but the Indian food at Sea Shell Havelock was outstanding — fresh, flavorful, and genuinely memorable.

05

Neil's Cove. A tucked-away cove accessible from Barefoot resort — calm, clear water and barely anyone else around.

"The Andamans surprised me. I knew the beaches would be beautiful — I didn't expect the history, the food, or the feeling of being somewhere that still feels genuinely remote. It's India, but unlike any India I'd seen before."
